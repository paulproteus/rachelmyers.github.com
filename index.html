March 2011 - Present (started as Associate Engineer)

Designed and built several pieces of a service-oriented architecture, including Be The Buyer and Comments Service.

A 'Be the Buyer' voting app was the first customer-facing app developed independently of the main rails app:

  • For a smooth user experience, all customer-facing app share sessions and sign in logic.
  • We collaborated with design to build the app responsively; the same views are work on all device resolutions and screen sizes.
  • All common logic and assets needed across apps are encapsulated in private gems that can be included anywhere; for example, admin user authentication and fetching product images at the right sizse and resolution.
  • We siloed the admin tools in an engine that is currently mounted in the customer facing app, but can easily be separated. Modularity was the rule.
  • For internal reporting we migrated data from the highly coupled models of the large app to a few small, modular models.

Comments Service, a backend service auto moderating comments throughout the site:

  • History of human moderations trained a baysian filter (Anakusa gem) that estimates a comment's similarity to good or bad comments.
  • Built to only know or care about comments so it can be consumed widely (sample comments, community posts, and product reviews)
  • Again, for internal reporting, we migrated data from confusing, coupled models to a simple, straightforward models.

Backbone-based mobile web:

  • To provide a better experience on phones, we built backbone models and views on top of Rails routes, controllers and models.

Solr-based categories:

  • The presentation-layer product categories became indexed solr queries based on product properties.
  • We additionally created a parsing tool to let merchandising create new categories quickly.
  • Solr-based categories were the technical down payment for filter-based shopping; filters somewhat simply added parameters to the search query.
  • We created a middleware rack app to quickly redirect old urls (way faster than rails).

Password security:
